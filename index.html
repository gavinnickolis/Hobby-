Writing as a Hobby

"How vain it is to sit down to write when you have not stood up to live" -Henry David Thoreau

Writing is a hobby that is beneficial in so many ways. It can help with your memory, diversify your creativity, and improve your own self-expression. And it can do so much more, so get ready to dive in!

Learn more
Tip 1: Read

Read, read, read. It is a lot harder to get the hang of writing if you don't first have any references. Try to think of what you would like to write, whether that be short story, novel, poems, etc. Then go out and find a book from a notable writer from the past that did it well. Write throughout reading to see your progress down the line!

Tip 2: Relearn

I know none of us miss school, but if could be handy to go back and get a full understanding of the things we may have forgot. Concepts like metaphors, similes and other figures of speech are ways to create impactful images that boost creativity. While Alliteratoins, consonance, and assonance enhance the creative sound and rhythms of your words.

Tip 3: Never Stop

The greatest way to get better is to well... Just write! As a beginning writer it can be daunting to look at that solemn blank page, but there is no one but you that can write what you want. Don't ignore those random ideas you think would be cool, even the bad ones. You never know what will trigger the better idea down the line, so keep a journal handy, or write in your phones notes app when you dont!
